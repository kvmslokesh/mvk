Do you find yourself confused or doubtful about the meaning of the terms DTCP and HMDA, which are commonly used in discussions related to the real estate industry? Do they appear similar to you, and you’re doubtful of the differences? If so, you’ve come to the right place. In this blog, we’ll look at why there are two distinct regulatory bodies governing the real estate industry and what separates them.

​HMDA and DTCP are two regulatory agencies that play a crucial role in the real estate industry in Hyderabad and across Telangana. Both agencies regulate land use and development and ensure that the real estate industry complies with the law. Although HMDA and DTCP have similar roles, there are some important differences between the two.

1. What is HMDA?

 

  • The Hyderabad Metropolitan Development Authority (HMDA) was established in 2008 by an Act (G.O.Ms.No.570 MA & UD (11) Dept., Dt.25.08.2008).
  • The Act was passed by the former Andhra Pradesh government, which was in power at the time.
  • After the separation of Andhra Pradesh and Telangana in 2014, the Hyderabad Metropolitan Development Authority (HMDA) came under the jurisdiction of the Government of Telangana.
  • The HMDA has a responsibility area of 7,257 Sq Km.
  • The HMDA has jurisdiction over 7 districts, 70 mandals, and 1032 villages.
  • This includes the Greater Hyderabad Municipal Corporation, which has 175 villages, and 40 Municipalities / Nagar Panchayats, which have 138 villages.
  • The remaining 719 villages are also under the jurisdiction of the HMDA.

The former Hyderabad Urban Development Authority (HUDA), Hyderabad Airport Development Authority (HADA), Cyberabad Development Authority (CDA), and Buddha Poornima Project Authority (BPPA) were combined to become HMDA.

HMDA plans, coordinates, supervises, promotes, and secures planned development in the Hyderabad Metropolitan Region. It works with local authorities, water supply & sewerage boards, and Infrastructure corporations.

2. Functions of HMDA

 

HMDA has several functions, including land acquisition, urban planning, and Infrastructure development. It is responsible for identifying and acquiring land for various development projects and ensuring that it is used appropriately. The authority plans and develops residential, commercial, and industrial areas and ensures that the Infrastructure is in place to support these areas.

HMDA also has a significant role in transport planning and development. It oversees the construction and maintenance of roads, flyovers, and bridges, and ensures that public transport systems are in place and operational. The authority also monitors traffic flow and makes recommendations for improvements.

 

Maintaining and managing the Hyderabad Metropolitan Development Fund, allocating funds based on local bodies’ plans and programmes for undertaking the development of amenities and infrastructure facilities, as well as monitoring and exercising financial control over budgetary allocations for development works made through it to various public agencies, local bodies, and other agencies.

Any movable or immovable property may be acquired through purchase, trade, gift, lease, mortgage, negotiated settlement, or any other legal means.

HMDA approved layouts

HMDA approved layouts refer to residential or commercial layouts that have been granted approval by the Hyderabad Metropolitan Development Authority (HMDA) in India.

Additionally, HMDA is responsible for environmental protection and conservation. It ensures that development activities are carried out environmentally and sustainably and that natural resources are preserved.


3. What is the HMDA Master plan

 

HMDA master plan refers to the comprehensive development plan created by the Hyderabad Metropolitan Development Authority (HMDA) for the Hyderabad metropolitan area. It outlines land use patterns, infrastructure development, transportation networks, and other important aspects of urban planning for the city’s sustainable growth. The HMDA master plan is periodically updated to accommodate changing needs and evolving priorities.

Master plans are used by state authorities to envision and establish regulations for the future development of cities. In this context, we will look at the HMDA 2031 Master plan, which outlines the development of a city with a population of approximately 1.84 crore people and a workforce of approximately 65 lakhs. The HMDA 2031 Master plan proposal was prepared in 2011, taking into account current trends at the time, and the city has since developed in accordance with the prescribed model. 4. What is DTCP?

 

The DTCP full form is the Directorate of Town and Country Planning, which is a government agency responsible for regulating land use and development in Telangana. The DTCP is responsible for ensuring that all land development activities in Telangana are in compliance with the Telangana Urban Areas (Development) Act, 1975, and the Telangana Building Rules, 2012.DTCP (Directorate of Town and Country Planning) is a government department that exists in various states of India. However, it is not present in every state of India. Each state has its own town and country planning department, which may or may not have the same name as DTCP.

5. Functions of DTCP

 

The Directorate of Town and Country Planning (DTCP) is an important part of Telangana’s urban planning and development. It is responsible for supervising the creation of master plans, development plans, and town planning schemes for urban areas. It also approves various development activities, such as layout approvals, building approvals, and land-use changes.

 

Additionally, it works closely with local authorities and other stakeholders to ensure that development plans align with the needs and aspirations of the community. DTCP is also responsible for ensuring the safety of buildings and structures, inspecting them for safety, approving building plans, and issuing occupancy certificates for completed structures.

6. HMDA and DTCP Regulations On Real Estate Industry

 

The real estate market in Hyderabad and Telangana is impacted by the regulations of HMDA and DTCP. Real estate developers must ensure that their projects comply with the rules set by the HMDA or DTCP, depending on their location. Failure to comply with the regulations can result in fines, penalties, and even the cancellation of the project. In Telangana, India.

Cost Consideration

Another important aspect to consider when choosing between an HMDA and DTCP-approved property is the cost. Generally, properties with HMDA approval are more expensive than those with DTCP approval. This is because HMDA-approved properties are usually located in prime areas with high demand, whereas DTCP-approved properties are located in areas that are still developing and may not yet have all the necessary infrastructure and amenities.

Requirements and Regulations

HMDA-approved properties may be subject to stricter regulations and requirements, such as larger minimum plot sizes, wider roads, and improved Infrastructure. This may result in increased construction costs and longer development times. DTCP-approved properties, on the other hand, may have less stringent regulations and requirements, making them more affordable and simpler to develop.

Infrastructure and Amenities

Another factor to consider is the level of Infrastructure and amenities in the surrounding area. HMDA-approved properties are typically located in areas that already have a high level of development, with good road networks, public transport, shopping centers, and other amenities. DTCP-approved properties, on the other hand, may be located in areas that are still developing and may not yet have all the necessary Infrastructure and amenities.

Ultimately, the choice between an HMDA and DTCP-approved property will depend on your individual needs and preferences. If you are looking for a property in a prime location with all the necessary Infrastructure and amenities, an HMDA-approved property may be the best option for you. However, if you are looking for a more affordable property in a developing area, then a DTCP-approved property may be the better choice.

Regional ring road Hyderabad master plan

The master plan behind the proposal of Regional Ring Road Hyderabad is to increase the connectivity of major cities, towns of various districts. It would be 6 lanes 340 kilometres long. It connects many National Highways and State Highways. Therefore, the transport between the cities of Telangana and other states is very easy and time-saving.

Portions of Regional Ring Road

Regional ring road Hyderabad has been divided into two portions.

They are:

Northern Portion:

The Northern half portion of the RRR is approximately 160 kilometers long and it’s estimated cost was Rs. 9500 cr ( when it was proposed). It covers Sangareddy, Narsapur, Toopran, Gajwel, Pragnapur, Bhuvanagiri and Choutuppal cities.

Southern Portion:

Similarly, the Southern half portion of the Regional Ring Road is approximately 182 kilometers long and it’s estimated cost was Rs. 6480 cr (when it was proposed). It covers Choutuppal, Ibrahimpatnam, Kandukur, Amangal, Chevella, Shankarpally and Sangareddy.
