Developments of Hyderabad – Upcoming projects in Hyderabad

Hyderabad is the best city to invest in and has seen rapid development in recent years, apart from other cities in India. Hyderabad is developing in all aspects as many Global companies are looking to invest in Hyderabad. Aside from the investment, Hyderabad developments, infrastructure, and facilities provided by the Government is attracting people to the city. The connectivity of Hyderabad is also good. More than 8 Highways are passing through Hyderabad. Both rail and air connections are also good. Apart from these, there are so many new upcoming projects in various Hyderabad locations, which will boost the opportunities for investors. Below are the latest Projects and news on Hyderabad.

 

Hyderabad Pharma city

Hyderabad pharma city is the world’s largest pharma hub. It is going to be constructed on more than 19000 acres. And the estimated budget of the pharma city is Rs.16,395 crores. The entire Pharma city is divided into four parts. They are 1. NIMZ Manufacturing Zone 2. Pharma University 3. R&D Centre 4. Pharma Township. 

Pharma City Hyderabad anticipates 1,50,000 direct employment and 5,00,000 indirect employment, with an estimated investment potential of 64,000 crores and an anticipated export potential of 58,000 crores per year. For more information about Hyderabad Pharma city please click here.

World trade Centre

The Kapil Group is building the multi-phase World Trade Centre in Hyderabad. The WTC Hyderabad will be India’s largest WTC structure. It may be found in the Raviryala Hardware Park.

The World Trade Centre (WTC), Shamshabad, will be completed by 2025. It will cover approximately 15 lakh square feet and employ between 5,000 and 8,000 people. According to sources, this Phase will see an investment of Rs. 500-750 crore.

WTC Hyderabad will be the world’s largest trade centre once it is completed. The current largest WTC is in China on 40 acres, whereas WTC Hyderabad will be built on 60 acres.

National Design Centre 

IT and Industries Minister Mr. KT Rama Rao requested Mr.Piyush Goyal, Union Minister of Consumer Affairs, Food, and Public Distribution, to establish the first National Design Center (NDC) in Hyderabad, for which the Central government received a detailed project report (DPR).

1000’s of Startups

Hyderabad, the capital of the southern Indian state of Telangana, is rapidly becoming a hub for startups. With a thriving IT industry and a large pool of talented engineers and entrepreneurs, the city is attracting the attention of investors and venture capitalists.

One of the key drivers of this growth is the government’s focus on promoting entrepreneurship and innovation. The state government has set up several initiatives and incubators to support and mentor young startups. Additionally, the city’s proximity to other major Indian cities such as Bangalore and Mumbai, makes it an ideal location for businesses looking to expand their reach. These companies have not only created jobs and wealth for the city but also inspired a new generation of entrepreneurs to follow in their footsteps. Overall, the startup ecosystem in Hyderabad is vibrant and growing, and with the right support and resources, the city has the potential to become a major player in the global startup scene.

Hyderabad Nagpur Industrial Corridor

The government of Telangana requested the Central Government to Develop Hyderabad – Nagpur Industrial corridor on the existing National Highway 44. Telangana Government requested to develop this corridor as Delhi – Mumbai Industrial Corridor, so that it can generate more employment and wealth. This industrial corridor should be 550 kms long. 

Bengaluru Hyderabad industrial Corridor

Telangana urged the centre to reconsider its decision to set up a Defence industrial corridor in Uttar Pradesh. As the government says both Bengaluru and Hyderabad have many defence public undertaking companies and small manufacturing units. So, it is ideal to establish the defence corridor in the existing Bengaluru – Hyderabad Industrial Corridor.

T-Hub

An environment eager for innovation is enabled and empowered by T-Hub. It is headquartered in Hyderabad, and it is the world’s largest innovation Hub.  T-Hub is responsible for leading India’s innovative environment, which fuels new products and business concepts. It follows the Triple Helix innovation concept that emphasizes contact and cooperation between business, academia, and government. Since its incorporation in 2015, it has given access to superior technology, talent, mentors, consumers, corporations, investors, and governmental organizations to more than 1100+ national and international businesses. These will lead to employment generation in Hyderabad. 

Police HeadquarterS

Telangana Police Headquarters is also known as Police Command Control Centre. Headquarters consists of 4 Towers & occupied 7 Acres of land with a budget of 585 Crores. Telangana Police is using advanced technology & it can access 9,25,000 CC cameras in the entire state. A Disaster Management System is also available in this tower.

New secretariat building 

The New Secretariat building of Telangana is getting built with an 800 Cr budget. It’s expected to be inaugurated by 17th February 2023. It has more additional facilities than the older one. CMO Office will be on the 7th Floor. 

The Secretariat building will be the highest (278 ft) of other heritage monuments like the Taj Mahal (240 ft), Qutub Minar (237 ft), Charminar (183 ft), Quli Qutub Shah’s Tomb (196 ft), and the Buddha statue (58 ft). 

 V- shaped Bridge

The V Shaped Bridge project aims to develop a park in the middle of PV Ghat and People’s Plaza, connected to Hussain Sagar via a transparent glass paneled bridge that simulates the feeling of walking on the waves of the lake. The park will be surrounded by popular attractions such as Lumbini Park and NTR Garden and is expected to draw a large number of visitors every day, especially on weekends and holidays, as they will also be able to visit the hanging bridge and experience its unique features.

Musi riverfront development

Telangana Government is planning to develop the Musi river into a Tourist spot. Musi riverfront development will be from ORR To Gandipet (47 kms) along the river and from Himayath Sagar to Bapu ghat (8km). There should be a Cycling track, skywalks and cable bridges on and along the riverside that grabs the tourist attraction.

Mir alam tank cable bridge 

After the successful completion of the Durgam Cheruvu cable bridge and its tourism response, HMDA is also planning to construct another cable bridge bigger than Durgam Cheruvu bridge. It reduces the traffic on the Bengaluru National Highway-Chintalmet route & increases tourism in Hyderabad. The Mir Alam Tank bridge, which will be 2.5 kilometers long and contains six lanes, would cost Rs. 220 crores. Aarvee Associates Pvt Ltd will handle it.

Kothwalguda eco-park

The largest aquarium, aviary, and boardwalk in India would be located in the Eco Park in Kothwalguda, according to the Municipal Administration and Urban Development Minister (MA&UD), KT Rama Rao. A further 75 acres of land at Gandipet will also be developed, according to the Minister who laid the cornerstone for the Eco Park at Kothwalguda and opened the Landscape Eco Park at Osman Sagar.

Telangana Shaheed memorial park

The original plan for the Telangana Martyr’s Memorial, set to be built on 3.26 acres of land next to Lumbini Park, was a concrete structure in the shape of a star. However, the vision of a local Telangana sculptor transformed the design and the memorial, which is now nearing completion, is a symbol of the universal tradition of honoring martyrs through a flame. The structure boasts a unique architectural design that leaves a lasting impression.

Ambedkar Statue

The state government will erect a 125-foot-tall bronze statue of B.R. Ambedkar close to NTR Gardens. It will be Ambedkar’s highest statue in existence. The government has approved Rs.150 crores for installing the statue on 11 acres at NTR Gardens, as well as a building of a meditation center, photo gallery, and conference room, among other amenities. 

Regional ring road Hyderabad

The government of Telangana is proposing the construction of the Regional Ring Road, a 6-lane, 330-kilometer-long (210-mile) route that will surround Hyderabad, India. The aim of the project is to enhance the present road network and add additional parts where lacking links were discovered, while also linking significant national highways such as NH 65, NH 44, NH 163, and NH 765.

RRR is divided into portions (South and North portions). The estimated budget of this project is Rs.17000 crores.

Serilingampally Tunnel

The Telangana government will construct India’s longest highway tunnel in Hyderabad. The tunnel is expected to be roughly 10 kilometers long. A four-lane tunnel will be built between Jubilee Hills Road No. 45 Junction to Banjara Hills Road No. 12 Junction. The route will feature the KBR Park connection to the NFCL Junction in Punjagutta. The Greater Hyderabad Municipal Corporation will be in charge of the tunnel’s construction. (GHMC). This tunnel will alleviate traffic congestion in the area.

Indore – Hyderabad Express Highway and Industrial Corridor

It is going to develop in two phases. First The Highway will develop then the industrial corridor.

Nitin Gadkari, the Union Minister for Road Transport and Highways, officially opened the 96 km long segment of the Hyderabad-Indore Economic Corridor, which makes up the majority of the new Telangana corridor.

National Highway 161 is a four-lane from Ramsanpalle to the Telangana and Maharashtra border via Mangalore for a cost of Rs. 2,926 crores. From Ramsanpalle to the outskirts of Hyderabad, the state’s remaining section of the corridor is still being built.

Hyderabad Metro Phase – II

The Telangana Chief Minister laid the foundation stone for the Airport Express Line on 9th December 2022. The project, is estimated to cost Rs. 6,250 crores. The Phase 2 project of Hyderabad Metro includes a new line and three extensions of existing lines, with a total length of 67 km. The new line will run 32 km, with 8 stations, and will connect the Mindspace Junction to the Rajiv Gandhi International Airport via the Outer Ring Road and Gachibowli.

Regional Rapid Transit System

The government of Telangana is planning to introduce a Regional Rapid Transit System (RRTS) in two corridors – Hyderabad-Warangal and Hyderabad-Vijayawada – following the success of the Hyderabad Metro Rail Limited (HMRL) in three other corridors. The RRTS will provide fast, safe and comfortable transportation at a reasonable cost for the intra-regional movement of passengers. To advance the project, officials from several Departments of Telangana will visit Delhi in February to examine the RRTS model being implemented in the National Capital Region (NCR), as well as developments made by other states such as Kerala and Odisha. The project will be costly and will necessitate significant inter-ministerial consultations and approval, with construction and funding to begin once approved. Telangana will establish a transport corporation akin to the National Capital Region Transport Corporation (NCRTC) to take over the RRTS routes and obtain central government financing.

Mumbai-Hyderabad Bullet Train Proposal

Mumbai and Hyderabad cities will connect by the projected Mumbai-Hyderabad High-Speed Rail Corridor. Along with the Mumbai-Ahmedabad line, it will be a vital link in India’s network of high-speed rail lines once it is completed.

 

Hyderabad Airport expansion

GMR is expanding the Hyderabad International Airport. The expansion will be done in different phases. Not only the Airport but also there are plans to develop surrounding areas like the European market. Expansion of the Airport consists of 6 domestic bus arrival terminals, Runways, Taxiways, and 24 exiting gates.
